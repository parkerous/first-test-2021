⚙️ Recommended Settings

  • Hold Based Sprint ON — Hold Shift to sprint: faster movement, farther dives, faster glides, higher jumps. Keep that sprint bar working.
  • Camera Following Ball OFF — Prevents dizziness and keeps your camera stable for fast plays.
  • Camera Angle Based Control ON — Balls go exactly where your crosshair points. Toggle with N — check for “CAM” under the crosshair.
  • Manual Jump — Press Space twice (1st for run-up, 2nd to jump).
  • Boom Jump — Press the 2nd Space exactly 0.48s after the first for max height (you’ll hear a sound + see a foot effect).

📏 Court Rules

Backrow (BR) Rule: If “BR” is under your crosshair, you’re in the backrow. Jumping and hitting the ball over from inside the 5m line is a fault and loses a point (applies to setters too).

🧭 Position Rules & Receiving

Positioning

  • Stay in your zone: Playing your position is critical for team play. Don’t crowd teammates or cause confusion on the court.

Defensive Receiving

  • 4–3 Power (LONGS / outside the 5m line): Use M1+M2 for high, steady passes that give your team time to set up a counterattack.
  • 2–1 Power (SHORTS / inside the 5m line): Use for short, close balls. Keeps the ball under control and stops it flying over the net.

Offensive Receiving (Covering Spikes)

  • Spread out slightly around your hitter while they attack.
  • Use low power to safely pick up blocked balls so you can recycle the play into a counterattack.

⚡ Hitter Rules

Mechanics & Controls

  • Manual Jump: Highly recommended for experienced players — lets you correct errors mid-play (early, late, or fake jumps).
  • Camera Angle Based Control ON — Essential for precise aiming. Adjust your crosshair up/down mid-air to exploit gaps or tool the blockers.
  • Top of Your Crosshair: Always keep it UNDER the boundary line of the direction you’re hitting toward, so you don’t hit out.
  • Jump Timing + Swing: There’s a 0.5s delay on a run-up jump. Don’t swing early while ascending — you’ll miss. Learn your peak height and swing there to beat late-jumping blockers.

Court Awareness & Strategy

  • Court Awareness: Study the enemy’s habits — do they jump early for quicks, stay deep for longs, crowd the net for shorts? Punish it.
  • Unpredictability: Mix approach angles, use glides, flick your camera, target different spots.
  • Ball Shadow: Lost the ball in the air? Look at its shadow on the floor. If it’s on or next to you — swing and pray. Tracking gets intuitive over time.

Positioning & Defense

  • Staying in Position: Vital for offense and defense — being in your spot keeps you ready for fast plays.
  • Blocking & Unblocking: Don’t bite on fake quicks; blind jumps wreck your backrow defense. Read the setter. When blocking, commit close to your antenna to close defensive gaps.

🙌 Setter Rules

General Setting Mechanics

  • Camera Angle Based Control: ALWAYS ON
  • Ground Set: Add +1 power vs a mid-air set.
  • Behind the 5m Line: Add +1 power to your standard set.
  • Distance Rule: The farther you are from your target hitter, the more power you need.

Middle Sets

  • Middle Wide: 9 power, aimed between the Outside and Middle positions.
  • Middle V1: Power depends entirely on tracking and matching the middle blocker’s peak jump height.
  • Middle Delay: 3 from Middle Square | 4 beside Middle Square | 5+ if you change your angle.
  • Middle Slide: Executed exactly like a Quick Opposite, but hit by the Middle Blocker.
